League of Legends

Introduction

 League of Legends (LoL) is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed and published by Riot Games. Since its release in 2009, LoL has grown to become one of the world’s most popular and influential esports titles, beloved for its strategic depth and diverse champions.



Gameplay

 Players compete as champions in teams of five, battling across strategic maps with the goal of destroying the enemy's Nexus. The game features tactical objectives, teamwork, and fast-paced combat. Players use unique abilities, cooperative strategies, and resource management to outplay their opponents.



Champions

 League of Legends boasts over 140 champions, each with distinct abilities and playstyles. Champions fulfill roles such as Assassin, Tank, Support, Mage, Marksman, and Fighter—allowing for versatile team compositions and dynamic gameplay.
  • Assassins: Burst damage and stealthy mobility.
  • Tanks: Durable front-liners who absorb damage.
  • Supports: Healers and crowd controllers aiding teammates.
  • Mages: High-damage spellcasters with area control.
  • Marksmen: Ranged physical damage dealers.
  • Fighters: Balanced damage dealers and bruisers.


Competitive Scene

 League of Legends is a leading esports title with a massive global competitive scene. The annual World Championship draws millions of viewers, with regional leagues and tournaments held worldwide. Pro players and teams battle for prestigious titles and multi-million dollar prize pools.
